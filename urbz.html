Urbz

urbz is an action-research-design collective based in Dharavi, Mumbai. Their core philosophy revolves under the idea of propogating user-generated cities, where the citizens play an active and important role in any change or developement in their city. Being in Dharavi, the focus of a lot of the work is in understanding the socio-spatial dynamics that exist within low-rise, high density “home grown” neighbourhoods. Their work has been inspired by thinkers like Jane Jacobs and Patrick Geddes.

Over my last few months at urbz, I’ve been worked on projects that have ranged from writing, graphic design and illustration to systems design, architecture, research and art. The interdisplinary nature of projects has posed many unique challenges that I’ve enjoyed working on.

Below are listed some projects I had the opportunity to work on.

Entekochi Urban Design Competition

The Entekochi Urban Design Competition is a project joinlty conducted by the Kochi Municipal Corporation and GIZ in consultation with Kochi Collective (urbz and Design Combine) to reimagine the Mullassery Canal Neighbourhood of Ernakulam, Kochi. urbz played a key role in conducting the initital stages of participatory research that involved several stakeholder meetings with locals residents. This material was then used to inform the competition’s design brief. Key responsibilities for this project included coming up with graphics, writing blog posts, designing and managing the competition website as well as technicalities of the competition process.

The Dharavi Weekly

The Dharavi Weekly is a project that seeks to document challenges people of Dharavi are facing through the Covid-19 Pandemic. The method of data collection involves taking phone interviews of local residents. This is then consolidated into weekly reports that are published on the urbz website. Part of my involvement in the project was in setting the visual tone and layout for furture reports to follow.

The Dharavi Mangroves

This was an exploration of the Dharavi mangroves and the relationship they have with the local fisherman (Koli) community of Dharavi. The project began with an excursion into the mucky waters to document the fishing process, problems the locals face and innovative solutions they have come up with. The result was a series of articles posted on the urbz website. Read them here.
